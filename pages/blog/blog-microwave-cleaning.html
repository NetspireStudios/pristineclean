How to Clean Your Microwave: 5 Easy Methods That Work

Pristine & Clean Team December 18, 2024 4 min read

A dirty microwave isn't just unsightlyâ€”it's unhygienic and can affect the taste of your food. Regular microwave cleaning is essential for maintaining a healthy kitchen environment. Here are 5 proven methods to clean your microwave using common household items.

Why Regular Microwave Cleaning Matters

Your microwave sees daily use, accumulating food splatters, grease, and odors over time. These buildups can:

  • Harbor harmful bacteria and germs
  • Create unpleasant odors that transfer to food
  • Reduce heating efficiency
  • Cause permanent staining and damage
  • Attract pests and insects

Quick Tip: Clean your microwave weekly to prevent buildup. Daily food spills should be wiped immediately to avoid hardening and staining.

Method 1: Steam Cleaning with Water

The simplest and most effective method for routine microwave cleaning:

What You'll Need:

  • 1 microwave-safe bowl
  • 1 cup of water
  • Clean cloth or paper towels

Steps:

  1. Fill the microwave-safe bowl with water
  2. Microwave on high for 2-3 minutes until steam forms
  3. Let it sit for 2 minutes to allow steam to loosen debris
  4. Carefully remove the hot bowl
  5. Wipe down interior surfaces with a damp cloth
  6. Remove and clean the turntable separately

Method 2: White Vinegar Power Clean

Perfect for removing stubborn stains and eliminating odors:

What You'll Need:

  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Microwave-safe bowl
  • Clean cloth

Steps:

  1. Mix equal parts water and white vinegar in bowl
  2. Microwave for 3-4 minutes on high power
  3. Let steam work for 5 minutes without opening door
  4. Remove bowl and wipe all surfaces thoroughly
  5. For tough stains, dip cloth in the vinegar solution

Safety First

Always use microwave-safe containers and be careful when removing hot liquids. Let the microwave cool before cleaning and unplug if necessary.

Method 3: Lemon Fresh Cleaning

Natural degreasing power with a fresh citrus scent:

What You'll Need:

  • 1 fresh lemon
  • 1 cup water
  • Microwave-safe bowl

Steps:

  1. Cut lemon in half and squeeze juice into water
  2. Place lemon halves in the bowl with water
  3. Microwave for 2-3 minutes
  4. Allow steam to work for 3 minutes
  5. Use lemon halves as scrubbers for tough spots
  6. Wipe clean with damp cloth

Method 4: Baking Soda Deep Clean

Ideal for tackling grease and burnt-on food:

What You'll Need:

  • 2 tablespoons baking soda
  • 1 cup warm water
  • Soft scrub brush or cloth

Steps:

  1. Create paste with baking soda and small amount of water
  2. Apply paste to stubborn stains and grease spots
  3. Let sit for 10-15 minutes
  4. Steam clean with remaining water for 2 minutes
  5. Scrub gently and wipe clean

Method 5: Dish Soap Solution

Perfect for general cleaning and regular maintenance:

What You'll Need:

  • 1-2 drops liquid dish soap
  • 1 cup warm water
  • Clean sponge or cloth

Steps:

  1. Mix dish soap with warm water in bowl
  2. Microwave solution for 1-2 minutes
  3. Let steam work for 2 minutes
  4. Dip cloth in soapy water and clean interior
  5. Rinse with clean damp cloth
  6. Dry thoroughly with clean towel

Professional Microwave Cleaning Tips

Pro Tips

  • Always remove the turntable and wash separately
  • Clean the exterior with appropriate cleaner for your microwave's finish
  • Don't forget to clean the door seals and hinges
  • Use a toothbrush for hard-to-reach crevices
  • Place an open box of baking soda inside overnight to absorb odors

What NOT to Use

  • Harsh abrasives that can scratch surfaces
  • Steel wool or scouring pads
  • Bleach or ammonia-based cleaners
  • Commercial oven cleaners
  • Metal objects for scraping

Maintaining a Clean Microwave

Prevention is key to keeping your microwave clean:

Daily Habits:

  • Cover food with microwave-safe lids or paper towels
  • Wipe spills immediately while they're still warm
  • Use lower power settings to prevent splattering
  • Keep a microwave-safe cover or plate handy

Weekly Maintenance:

  • Steam clean with water method
  • Wash and sanitize turntable
  • Wipe exterior and control panel
  • Check and clean air vents
